Ion Formation Visualizer

See how atoms gain or lose electrons to form ions with stable electron configurations

Visualization Guide

  • Colored shells: Electron orbitals (red = 1st shell, green = 2nd shell, blue = 3rd shell, etc.)
  • Colored spheres: Electrons (color matches their shell)
  • Central sphere: Atomic nucleus
  • + or - symbols: Ion charge indicator (appears only for ions)

About Ions

Ions are atoms that have gained or lost electrons, resulting in a net electrical charge.

  • Cations are positively charged ions that have lost electrons. They are indicated with a + symbol.
  • Anions are negatively charged ions that have gained electrons. They are indicated with a - symbol.

Atoms tend to gain or lose electrons to achieve a stable electron configuration, typically with a filled outer shell (octet rule).
