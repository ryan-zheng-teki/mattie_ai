How to Read the Atom View

Nucleus

The center of the atom where protons and neutrons live!

Shells

Colored rings where electrons orbit - each shell can only hold so many electrons.

Electrons

Tiny particles that buzz around the nucleus in specific shells. Different colors show which shell they belong to.

Fun Facts About Atoms

If an atom were the size of a football stadium, the nucleus would be the size of a pea in the center!

Electrons move incredibly fast, almost at the speed of light, as they orbit the nucleus.

Despite being much smaller than protons, electrons have the same amount of electric charge (but opposite).

The number of electrons in an atom's outermost shell determines many of its chemical properties!

Atoms are so small that a line of 1 million atoms would only be about 1 millimeter long!
